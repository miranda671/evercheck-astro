## 1. What is CGM and how does it work?

    

A Continuous Glucose Monitor is a wearable medical device that tracks glucose levels in real time, typically measuring interstitial fluid every 1 to 15 minutes. Unlike traditional finger-prick blood glucose meters, CGM provides a **continuous data stream** —revealing trends, spikes, and patterns that single-point readings simply cannot capture.

    

The core components of a CGM system include:
    

      - **Sensor filament** —A tiny enzyme-coated electrode inserted under the skin to measure glucose in interstitial fluid
      - **Transmitter** —A wireless module that sends raw sensor data to a smartphone or dedicated reader via Bluetooth
      - **Adhesive patch** —Medical-grade adhesive that keeps the sensor securely attached for up to 14 days
      - **Applicator device** —A single-use insertion mechanism that places the sensor filament with minimal discomfort
      - **Mobile app or reader** —The software interface that displays glucose readings, trends, and alerts
    

    

Modern CGM systems like the EverCheck achieve **factory calibration**, meaning users no longer need finger-prick calibration. The sensor is pre-calibrated during manufacturing, and the algorithm compensates for individual physiological variations over the wear period.

    

## 2. The CGM market: size, growth, and opportunity

    

The global CGM market was valued at approximately **$6.8 billion in 2024** and is projected to reach $16.3 billion by 2032, growing at a CAGR of over 11%. Several factors are driving this growth:

    

      - **Expanding beyond diabetes** —CGM is increasingly adopted by athletes, wellness consumers, and metabolic health programs, opening entirely new market segments
      - **Regulatory tailwinds** —Reimbursement policies in the US, Europe, and parts of Asia are expanding coverage for CGM devices
      - **Over-the-counter availability** —FDA clearance for OTC CGM devices is creating new direct-to-consumer channels
      - **Manufacturing maturity** —The technology supply chain has matured, making it feasible for new brands to enter the market through OEM and SKD partnerships
    

    

> 💡 **Key insight:** The fastest-growing CGM markets outside the US include Germany, Japan, Saudi Arabia, and Brazil —each with strong government healthcare procurement programs and growing diabetes prevalence. Brands that establish local manufacturing or assembly presence in these regions gain significant competitive advantages in public tenders.
